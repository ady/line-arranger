      About Line Arranger

      Long before "remix culture" had a name, writers and musicians were cutting up their own words and putting them back together at random to see what showed up. The novelist William S. Burroughs, working with the artist Brion Gysin in the 1950s and 60s, sliced up pages of text and reassembled the strips in new orders — the "cut-up technique" — treating chance as a way to find images and connections a careful, linear draft would never produce.

      David Bowie picked up the same method for his lyrics, famously cutting sentences into individual lines or words, shuffling them, and building verses from whatever order they landed in — a way of short-circuiting his own habits as a writer. Genesis P-Orridge, of Throbbing Gristle and Psychic TV, used cut-up techniques across music, tape collage, and performance art in a similar spirit, treating disruption and randomness as a creative tool rather than a problem to solve.

      This tool is a small digital version of that same idea: get your raw lines or words down, then let randomness and rearrangement do some of the work for you.

      How to use it

      • Paste a block of text into Fragments and click Add All (it splits one line per row), or add a single line/word with the quick-add field.
      • Click Shuffle to randomise the order of your fragments — a good way to notice lines you'd otherwise skip past.
      • Drag fragments into the Arrangement panel to build your piece, and drag to reorder within either list.
      • Double-click any line's text, in Fragments or the Arrangement, to edit it in place.
      • Everything autosaves in your browser. Use Export arrangement (.txt) to download your finished piece, or Save progress for later (.json) to save and reload an in-progress session.
